Overall Progress 0 / 24
Check off items as you complete each area.
🎯 Business and Scope
0 / 4
  • The business problem, intended users, and use cases are clearly defined.
  • Success criteria and release gates are documented.
  • The limits of the AI system are understood, including what it should not be used for.
  • A fallback or non-AI path exists if the AI output is wrong or unavailable.
🔒 Data, Privacy, and Compliance
0 / 4
  • Data sources are approved and appropriate for production use.
  • Sensitive, personal, confidential, or regulated data is identified and protected.
  • Retention, logging, access, and consent rules are defined.
  • Legal, privacy, compliance, or policy review is complete where required.
🛡️ Security and Risk Controls
0 / 4
  • The application has been reviewed for prompt injection, data leakage, misuse, and access risks.
  • Authentication, authorization, and environment secrets are secured.
  • Tool use, API actions, and downstream integrations are restricted to what is necessary.
  • A clear escalation path exists for harmful, unsafe, or incorrect outputs.
✅ Quality and Testing
0 / 4
  • The AI has been tested on realistic scenarios, edge cases, and failure cases.
  • Accuracy, hallucination risk, latency, and consistency have been evaluated.
  • Human review is built in for high-risk use cases.
  • User acceptance testing or pilot feedback has been completed.
🚀 Operations and Launch Readiness
0 / 4
  • Monitoring, logging, alerts, and performance tracking are in place.
  • Versioning, rollback, and incident response plans are defined.
  • Cost controls, rate limits, and usage guardrails are configured.
  • Support owners are identified for business, technical, and operational issues.
📋 Governance and Continuous Improvement
0 / 4
  • The model, prompts, tools, data sources, and known limitations are documented.
  • Approvals and sign-off are complete.
  • Users are informed when content is AI-generated and how to report issues.
  • A post-launch review process is in place to monitor quality, drift, and risk over time.

Ready for production only when the system is not just impressive in a demo, but dependable in real-world use.
